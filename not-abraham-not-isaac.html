Not Abraham, Not Isaac

Walk of Suffering

Story in Genesis 22 – when Abraham obeyed the LORD by sacrificing his son Isaac, holds so many parallels with the story of the suffering and death of Jesus of Nazareth.

Son Whom You Love

The chapter begins when the LORD told Abraham:

"Take your son, your only son Isaac, whom you love, and go to the land of Moriah, and offer him there as a burnt offering on one of the mountains of which I shall tell you." – Genesis 22:2

This parallels the voice from heaven when Jesus was baptized and about to begin His ministry:

"This is my Son, whom I love; with him I am well pleased." – Matthew 3:17 (NIV)

Divided into three parts, the Lord’s command to Abraham mirrors the voice from heaven after Jesus' baptism in the following ways:

  • "Take your son, your only son Isaac" matches with "This is my son". Abraham had two biological sons, but only Isaac was the son of the covenant.
  • "Whom you love" matches "Whom I love".
  • “Go to the land of Moriah, and offer him...” aligns with the part “With whom I am well pleased” — God’s pleasure in Jesus came through His obedience, especially in submitting to be crucified.

The Destination

In the Bible, God told Abraham to go to one of the mountains in the land of Moriah. Many believe this is the same place where Solomon later built the Temple (2 Chronicles 3:1), and near the location of Golgotha where Jesus was crucified.

3 Days to Mount Moriah

Abraham took three days to reach Mount Moriah. This parallels the approximately three-year ministry of Jesus, from baptism to crucifixion.

Two Young Men and A Donkey

Abraham went with Isaac, two young men, and a donkey. This parallels Jesus telling two disciples to fetch a donkey:

"As they approached Jerusalem and came to Bethphage on the Mount of Olives, Jesus sent two disciples, saying to them, “Go to the village ahead of you, and at once you will find a donkey tied there, with her colt by her. Untie them and bring them to me. If anyone says anything to you, say that the Lord needs them, and he will send them right away.”" – Matthew 21:1-3 (NIV)

The Bible doesn’t say whether Isaac rode a donkey, but a grieving father might have let his son ride. This matches Jesus riding into Jerusalem on a donkey.

Stay and Watch

Abraham told the two young men to stay behind while he and Isaac went alone. This parallels Jesus telling His disciples in Gethsemane:

"Remain here and watch with me." – Matthew 26:38

After Gethsemane, Jesus began His journey of suffering, alone with the Father.

Similar Age

Jesus and Isaac may have been around the same age when they were to be sacrificed. Jesus was around 33 years old. The Bible doesn’t specify Isaac’s age, but Sarah died at 127 (Genesis 23:1), making Isaac around 37 or younger. Jewish tradition holds Sarah died from shock after the event, suggesting Isaac may have been 37 years old.

Carry The Wood

Jesus and Isaac both carried the wood meant for their sacrifice.

No Resistance

Both submitted without resistance. Isaac, strong enough to carry wood, could have resisted a 110-year-old man, but didn’t. Jesus also did not resist arrest or crucifixion.

Blessing for the Whole World

After nearly sacrificing Isaac, God said:

"And in your offspring shall all the nations of the earth be blessed, because you have obeyed my voice." – Genesis 22:18

This points to Jesus, Abraham’s descendant, through whom the world is truly blessed.

Covenant of the Pieces

Earlier, God made a covenant with Abraham. Normally, both parties walk between dead animals to signify death upon breaking the covenant. But God alone passed through while Abraham slept:

"And when the sun had gone down and it was dark, behold, a smoking fire pot and a flaming torch passed between these pieces." – Genesis 15:17

This foreshadows the Father sacrificing His own Son instead of Abraham sacrificing Isaac.

According to the Book of Jubilees and Jewish tradition, this event occurred the same day and time as the first Passover. In Exodus:

"You shall keep it until the fourteenth day of this month, when the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill their lambs at twilight." – Exodus 12:6

Josephus, a historian born 37 AD, wrote:

"...their sacrifices were performed from the ninth hour till the eleventh..." – Josephus, Wars of the Jews, Book 6, Chapter 9, Section 3

Both of the writing shows when the passover lambs were sacrificed on the Temple.

These events all happened on 2 specific hour on the 14th of month Nisan (Jewish calendar):

  • Around 3 PM – Arguably Abraham prepared the animals, lambs were sacrificed, and Jesus died.
  • On twilight before sunset – Abraham fell asleep and darkness came, the Passover sacrifice ended, and Jesus was buried.

The Sorrowed Suffering Father

Perhaps the writer of Genesis wanted to show the grief of God the Father, whose heart was torn when offering His Son. We can only guess Abraham’s sorrow walking with Isaac for three days. No one can fully grasp the heartbreak of leading a beloved son up a hill for sacrifice.

But it was not Isaac. It was Jesus. Jesus – who felt the cat of nine tails shred His skin and flesh. Who wore acrown of thorns driven into His skull. Who was stripped, mocked, and humiliated in public. It was His hands and feet that were pierced by nails – not Isaac’s. This is no myth. It is a historical fact: a man named Jesus of Nazareth endured one of the most brutal tortures ever carried out in that time and place.

And it was not Abraham..
