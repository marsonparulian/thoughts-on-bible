No Faith is Required

Articles in this collection are a compilation of thoughts related to the Bible. These articles are not intended to promote the belief that the Bible is a form of divine revelation. Instead, they aim to explore the Bible as an ancient text written by many people over hundreds of years, across various cultures and political systems.

These articles do not seek to advocate for the Christian faith, but rather to observe patterns, symbolism, and messages conveyed through stories in the Bible, written over a significant span of time.

Featured Articles

Acts 16: Evangelism

An exploration of different approaches to evangelism through Paul's ministry, including his encounters with Lydia and others. This study examines how Paul used preaching, divine power, and personal example to spread the Gospel.

Baptism Throughout The Bible

A comprehensive look at the significance of baptism in Christianity, exploring its Greek origins, symbolic meaning, and its role in spiritual transformation and renewal across different cultures.

Similarities between Jesus and Aaron

An insightful comparison between Jesus and Aaron, exploring their roles as spiritual leaders and how Aaron's priesthood prefigures aspects of Christ's ministry. The study examines their positions as second persons in their respective contexts.

Similarities between Maria and Miriam

A fascinating parallel study between Mary (Maria), the mother of Jesus, and Miriam, Moses' sister. Based on teachings from Dr. Rita Wahyu, this article explores the connections between these two important biblical women.
